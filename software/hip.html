hip: homogenization of multi-wavelength images
Multi-\(\lambda\) image homogenization pipeline

Idea

A common idea in multi-\(\lambda\) astronomy is to compare images of the same physical space, i.e galaxies, at different wavelengths. These images are usually taken with different instruments of different field-of-view and resolution. In order to do a consistent comparision, we need to align all images to a common world-coordinate-system, degrade them to a common lower resolution, and resample them on the same pixel grid. The homogenization process consists in three main steps:

 1. Reprojecting the images to the same orientation
 2. Degrading the images to the same spatial resolution
 3. Regridding the images to the same pixel size

Implementation

The HIP pipeline, written initially by L.Pantoni and maintained by me, is a multi-\(\lambda\) homogenization pipeline used within the IMEGIN collaboration to homogenize NIKA2 and Dustpedia data. Other than the afore-mentioned steps, the pipeline also offers the possiblity to:

 1. Subtract background and foreground
 2. Mask bright foreground stars
 3. Subtract CO(2-1) emission in the 1.15 mm continuum
 4. Propagate uncertainties through a Monte-Carlo loop

All details are available on the repository's documentation section (restricted to IMEGIN members, for now). The pipeline is controlled by a user-defined environment file, and allows to homogenize multiple bands for multiple galaxies at once.
