hip: homogenization of IMEGIN photometry
Multi-\(\lambda\) image homogenization pipeline

Idea

A common idea in multi-\(\lambda\) astronomy is to compare images of the same physical space, i.e galaxies, at different wavelengths. These images are usually taken with different instruments of different field-of-view and resolution. In order to do a consistent comparision, we need to allign all images to a common world-coordinate-system, degrade them to a common lower resolution, and resample them on the same pixel grid. The homogenization process consists in three main steps:

 1. Reprojecting the images to the same orientation
 2. Degrading the images to the same spatial resolution
    (as presented in G. Aniano 2011)
 3. Regridding the images to the same pixel size

The HIP pipeline, written initially by L.Pantoni and maintained by me, is a multi-\(\lambda\) homogenization pipeline used within the IMEGIN collaboration to homogenize NIKA2 and Dustpedia data and allow the construction of pixel-by-pixel SED models of nearby extended galaxies. Other than the afore-mentioned steps, the pipeline also offers the possiblity to:

 1. Subtraction of background and foreground
    (i.e Galactic cirrus, CIB, etc.)
 2. Masking bright foreground stars
    (point source catalog by Cutri et al. 2003)
 3. Subtraction of CO(2-1) emission in the 1.15 mm continuum
    (as presented in Drabek et al. 2012)
 4. Propagation of uncertainties through a Monte-Carlo loop

Implementation

All details are available in the repository's documentation section (for now, restricted to IMEGIN members).

The pipeline is controlled by a user-defined environment file, and allows to homogenize multiple bands for multiple galaxies at once. The users has to input, among other things, the list of bands to homogenize, the target band, error maps (if available), and parameters to control the setting of the different sections of the pipeline.
